A Call to Scientists: Support Open Access

Knowledge Should Be Free

Open Access (OA) means that research outputs—papers, data, code—are immediately and permanently available online for anyone, anywhere, to read, download, copy, and build upon without price or permission barriers. As scientists, we pride ourselves on advancing understanding for the benefit of humanity. Yet every day, paywalls lock away the discoveries we make, slowing progress and widening global inequities.

Why Every Researcher Should Champion Open Access

  • Greater Impact: OA articles are cited more often, accelerating scientific conversation and innovation.
  • Global Equity: Researchers in low- and middle-income countries gain equal access to cutting-edge knowledge.
  • Public Good: Taxpayers fund much of our work; they deserve to see the results without paying again.
  • Transparency & Trust: Freely shared data and methods enhance reproducibility and public confidence in science.

What You Can Do Today

  1. Publish in OA venues: Choose journals that offer immediate open access, or society journals with transparent OA options.
  2. Retain your rights: Use author addenda or publish under a Creative Commons license so you—and others—can share your work freely.
  3. Deposit your work: Post preprints to disciplinary servers (arXiv, bioRxiv, PsyArXiv, etc.) and final manuscripts in institutional or subject repositories.
  4. Review & edit for OA: Volunteer your time for open-access journals that waive fees for authors who lack funds.
  5. Advocate: Talk to your department head, scholarly society, and library about redirecting subscription funds to sustainable OA models.
  6. Teach OA: Encourage students and mentees to practice open science from day one.

Debunking Common Myths

Myth: “OA journals are low quality.”
Fact: Many OA journals (e.g., PLOS Biology, eLife, Nature Communications) maintain rigorous peer review and high impact factors.

Myth: “Author fees make OA unethical.”
Fact: Fee waivers exist for authors without grants, and diamond OA journals charge neither readers nor authors, supported by universities or societies.

Myth: “We can’t afford to flip our society journal.”
Fact: Libraries collectively spend billions on subscriptions; reallocating a fraction can underwrite equitable, open publishing platforms.

Take the Pledge

I will:

  • Make my future research openly available whenever possible.
  • Support colleagues who choose OA publishing.
  • Advocate for fair, transparent, and sustainable scholarly communication within my institution and discipline.

Science thrives when knowledge flows freely. Join the movement—support Open Access today.
